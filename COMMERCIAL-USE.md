## Commercial Uses

Commercial use includes using Socket, its plugins, skills, documentation,
examples, prompts, MCP servers, hooks, packaged artifacts, or derived works
for a business, employer, client, paid product, hosted service, commercial
training, paid consulting, product development, model training, evaluation,
or any other activity intended to generate revenue, reduce business costs,
or provide commercial advantage.

These are commercial uses:

- A contractor, consultant, freelancer, sole proprietor, agency, studio, or
  other self-employed person using Socket while selling services.
- A person using Socket to do work for an employer, whether or not the
  employer explicitly approved that use.
- A startup using Socket before or after revenue.
- A business using Socket internally, even without redistributing Socket.
- A business using Socket content, skills, examples, prompts, metadata, or
  documentation to train, evaluate, benchmark, design, or improve software,
  AI systems, developer tools, agents, models, products, services, or
  internal workflows.
- A nonprofit, charity, foundation, trade group, association, or similar
  organization using Socket to provide services, sell work, run operations,
  support paid programs, reduce organizational costs, or otherwise conduct
  commerce.
- A paid course, book, training program, workshop, or consulting engagement
  using Socket or Socket-derived material.
- A platform, marketplace, tool vendor, model provider, enterprise agent
  program, or product team using Socket to build, improve, package, support,
  or operate their own commercial offering.

If the use benefits a business, client, employer, product, commercial
service, paid program, model, or revenue-seeking organization, treat it as
commercial use and contact Gale for licensing.

## Noncommercial Uses

Noncommercial use includes personal study, private experimentation, hobby
projects, amateur pursuits, and public-knowledge research without anticipated
commercial application.

Educational, public research, public safety or health, environmental
protection, charitable, and government uses may be permitted by the public
license only when the use is genuinely noncommercial. A nonprofit, charity,
foundation, association, or similar organization using Socket to do
operational work, provide services, support paid programs, reduce costs, or
conduct commerce is making commercial use and needs a separate written
commercial license from Gale.
